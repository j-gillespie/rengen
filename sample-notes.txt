SUPERSONIC ROCKET
--------------------------------
WHAT'S NEW
- Added adjustable thrust modulator with three settings: low, medium, and high. Replaces the fixed-output nozzle from v3.0.x.
- New quick-release fuel canister mechanism. Swap canisters in under 10 seconds without tools.
- Overhauled targeting reticle UI. Reticle now shows wind speed and estimated trajectory arc in real time.
- Reduced ignition lag from 2.4s to 0.8s. Firmware change, no hardware required.
- Added compatibility with ACME-brand Parachute Recovery System (sold separately).

BUGS FIXED
- #4801: Fuel gauge displayed incorrect reading after partial refill. Fixed calculation in firmware.
- #4823: Launch button unresponsive at temperatures below 20░F. Root cause was stiff polymer in trigger housing; replaced with cold-rated material.
- #4788: Targeting reticle flickered when device was tilted more than 45 degrees. Fixed gyroscope calibration routine.
- #4756: Parachute recovery port cover would not reseat after opening. Redesigned latch mechanism.

KNOWN ISSUES
- #4901: Thrust modulator setting resets to "low" after firmware update. Workaround: manually reset preferred thrust level after updating.
- #4912: Real-time trajectory arc may display incorrectly at wind speeds above 40 mph. No workaround at this time; fix targeted for v3.1.1.

SYSTEM REQUIREMENTS
- Compatible with ACME Fuel Canisters Type-R and Type-X only. Type-M canisters (discontinued 2022) are not supported.
- Firmware update requires ACME Device Manager v2.0 or later (Windows 10/11, macOS 12+).
- Operating temperature range: -10░F to 130░F.
- Minimum operator age: 18 years.

INSTALLATION / UPDATE
- Take components out of shipping box, and carefully remove packaging material.
- Insert the ōUö end of the tubular rocket body (B) into the opening of the cone (C), and tighten all screws. 
- Insert the ōDö end of the rocket body into the open side of the tail piece (T), and tighten all screws on the tail housing. Note: Take care not to bend or break any of the fins (F1, F2, F3, F4) that are affixed to the tail piece.
- Pack the rocket parachute into the aft compartment and close the compartment door (D).
- From the opening in the tail piece (O), pull out a length of fuse; each 5 inches of fuse allows an approximate 10-second delay before launch time.
- Connect operator harness (H) to rocket body using the metal hooks (H1, H2) on the rocket.

SUPPORT NOTES
- Field reports of the #4912 trajectory issue should be logged via the ACME support portal with wind speed reading at time of issue.
- Warranty covers manufacturing defects only. Damage resulting from use with non-ACME fuel canisters is not covered.

Now ships with a separate Just-in-Time (JIT) parachute for rocket operators.
